A Framework for Building Loss-Resistant Healthcare Financing at Scale

Healthcare financing sits at the intersection of two critical imperatives: expanding access to medical care and maintaining rigorous financial discipline. This white paper presents PaySick's comprehensive framework for underwriting risk and managing adverse selection in healthcare lending across South Africa and five global expansion markets.

The analysis addresses how structural asymmetries in healthcare financing, including information gaps between borrowers and lenders, moral hazard in treatment selection, and cyclical economic pressures, can be systematically mitigated through a multi-layered risk architecture. The PaySick Shield Framework integrates borrower verification, procedure-level risk scoring, dynamic pricing, portfolio diversification, and loss-absorption reserves into a unified system targeting net loss rates below 2% across economic cycles.

Spanning 11 sections, the paper examines population segmentation, procedure-specific economics, scenario-based stress testing, regulatory considerations, and a phased global expansion roadmap, offering institutional and investor audiences a rigorous foundation for evaluating PaySick's risk management architecture.

Key Findings

What the research reveals

Market Opportunity
84% of South Africans lack medical aid coverage

The structural gap between healthcare need and financing availability creates a vast, underserved market. PaySick targets the intersection of clinical urgency and financial exclusion where traditional credit products fail.

Risk Architecture
5-layer Shield Framework targeting < 2% net loss

Rather than relying on a single underwriting gate, the Shield Framework distributes risk mitigation across five structural layers, from individual scoring through to portfolio-level capital reserves, creating defence in depth.

Population Segments
Segment 1: 9.7M medical aid holders
Segment 2: 9.9M uninsured OOP users

Two distinct borrower populations with different risk profiles, repayment behaviours, and healthcare utilisation patterns. The dual-segment approach enables targeted underwriting and diversified portfolio construction.

Global Expansion
5 markets across 4 continents

India, United Kingdom, Australia, Kenya, and the Philippines each present validated market conditions for the PaySick model: significant healthcare financing gaps, supportive regulatory environments, and large addressable populations.

Procedure Economics
Dental, ophthalmology & cosmetic surgery: highest-margin verticals

Elective and semi-elective procedures with predictable cost structures and high patient commitment deliver the strongest risk-adjusted returns. These verticals form the foundation of early portfolio construction.

Default Performance
1.6% cumulative default over 25 years globally

Healthcare loans structurally outperform general consumer credit. The combination of treatment completion incentive, provider relationship continuity, and procedure-specific repayment motivation creates a natural loss-suppression mechanism.
