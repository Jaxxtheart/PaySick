PURPOSE OF THIS DISCLOSURE

This Licensing & Regulatory Disclosure sets out the regulatory status, licensing position, and perimeter limitations applicable to PaySick (Pty) Ltd ("PaySick"). It is intended to provide transparency to customers, healthcare providers, partners, regulators, and the general public.

This document must be read together with the PaySick Terms of Service and Privacy Policy.

1. CORPORATE STATUS

PaySick (Pty) Ltd is a private company incorporated in the Republic of South Africa.

2. NO CREDIT, LENDING, OR BANKING LICENCE

PaySick is not:

  • A bank or mutual bank;
  • A registered credit provider;
  • A lender, financier, or deposit-taking institution;
  • A provider of incidental or revolving credit.

PaySick does not provide loans, advances, overdrafts, or credit facilities as contemplated under the National Credit Act, 34 of 2005 (NCA).

PaySick facilitates deferred payment arrangements for specific healthcare services through fixed, non-revolving Payment Plans.

3. FINANCIAL REGULATORY PERIMETER

Accordingly:

  • PaySick is not registered as a credit provider under the NCA;
  • PaySick is not licensed or supervised by the South African Reserve Bank (SARB) as a bank;
  • PaySick does not render financial advice or intermediary services under the Financial Advisory and Intermediary Services Act, 37 of 2002 (FAIS).

Where PaySick integrates with regulated third parties (including banks, payment service providers, or identity verification providers), those parties operate under their own regulatory licences and supervisory frameworks.

4. PAYMENT PROCESSING AND THIRD-PARTY LICENSING

Payment processing, debiting, and settlement services are performed by licensed and regulated payment service providers. PaySick does not itself:

  • Hold or store full card details;
  • Perform regulated clearing or settlement activities;
  • Act as a payment system operator.

5. NO REGULATORY ENDORSEMENT OR RELIANCE

Use of PaySick does not imply:

  • Regulatory approval;
  • Licensing endorsement;
  • Prudential supervision.

Users and Providers acknowledge that they do not rely on PaySick as a licensed financial institution.

6. REGULATORY CHANGE AND RESERVATION OF RIGHTS

If any regulatory authority determines that any aspect of the PaySick Service requires licensing, registration, or authorisation, PaySick reserves the right to:

  • Modify or discontinue affected features;
  • Suspend or terminate Payment Plans;
  • Obtain appropriate licences where legally and commercially viable.
