PaySick Provider Billing Agreement

Version 2.0 — Please read carefully and complete the signature section below.

1. PARTIES

This Billing Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into between PaySick (Pty) Ltd ("PaySick") and the healthcare provider identified in the signature block below ("Provider").

2. PURPOSE

PaySick facilitates the payment of tariff gaps between what a patient's medical scheme pays and what the Provider charges. This Agreement governs the terms under which PaySick will settle gap amounts on behalf of patients ("Patients") treated by the Provider.

3. PROVIDER OBLIGATIONS

  • Provider must submit accurate invoices within 30 days of the procedure date.
  • Provider must submit the Explanation of Benefits (EOB) from the relevant medical scheme within 30 days of scheme payment.
  • Provider agrees not to charge Patients for amounts exceeding the Provider's standard tariff, as disclosed at time of treatment.
  • Provider's invoiced amount must not exceed 300% of the applicable NHRPL rate without prior written agreement from PaySick.
  • Provider must maintain HPCSA registration and immediately notify PaySick of any lapse, suspension, or disciplinary proceedings.

4. PAYOUT STRUCTURE

  • Provisional Payout: 80% of the approved facilitation ceiling will be paid within 2 business days of the procedure date.
  • Final Payout: The balance (up to 20% of the approved ceiling) will be settled within 5 business days of EOB receipt and reconciliation.
  • If the Provider's invoice exceeds the approved facilitation ceiling, the excess amount will be withheld and the Provider must pursue the balance directly with the Patient.

5. BILLING CAP

Provider agrees that for all PaySick-facilitated procedures, the maximum billable amount will not exceed 3 times the applicable NHRPL rate, unless a higher cap has been agreed in writing.

6. SUSPENSION

PaySick reserves the right to suspend this Agreement and withhold payouts if the Provider is found to be submitting fraudulent, inflated, or duplicate invoices, or if the Provider's HPCSA registration lapses.

7. GOVERNING LAW

This Agreement is governed by the laws of the Republic of South Africa. Any disputes shall be resolved through mediation before proceeding to litigation.
