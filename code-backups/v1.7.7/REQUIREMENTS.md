# Requirements & Specifications — PaySick v1.7.7

**Version**: 1.7.7
**Date**: 2026-04-20

Carries forward all requirements from v1.7.6 with the following additions.

---

## New Requirements

### Investor Deck — Direct Balance Sheet Funding Model

| ID | Requirement | Priority |
|----|-------------|----------|
| DECK-13 | The investor deck must reflect that PaySick funds every patient arrangement directly from its own balance sheet, with no third-party lenders or marketplace | Must Have |
| DECK-14 | The Solution slide must describe "PaySick Direct Funding" in place of any marketplace or multi-partner model | Must Have |
| DECK-15 | The Product / How It Works timeline must show Shield approval and PaySick disbursement steps, with no marketplace or partner-selection step | Must Have |
| DECK-16 | The Business Model slide must present three revenue streams: Patient Service Fee (60%), Provider Service Fee (25%), Late Payment Income (15%) | Must Have |
| DECK-17 | The Business Model unit economics must use R8,000 average arrangement size and R800 revenue per arrangement | Must Have |
| DECK-18 | The Competitive Advantages table must include "Direct Balance Sheet Funding" as a PaySick-only capability | Must Have |
| DECK-19 | The Defensibility slide must include a "Vertically Integrated Capital" moat card replacing "Marketplace Neutrality" | Must Have |
| DECK-20 | The Roadmap Q4 2026 milestone must be "National Roll-Out / 200 providers" | Must Have |

### Investor Deck — Updated Capital Ask

| ID | Requirement | Priority |
|----|-------------|----------|
| DECK-21 | Part 1 of the funding ask must be R20M (increased from R15M) to reflect direct balance sheet funding of all patient arrangements | Must Have |
| DECK-22 | The total capital ask must be R30M (R20M balance sheet capital + R10M operational equity) | Must Have |
| DECK-23 | The Part 1 facility must be described as "Balance Sheet Arrangement Capital" (not "Disbursement Facility") | Must Have |
| DECK-24 | The Part 1 description must state ~4× capital turns per year (R8K avg × 3-month term = 4× velocity) | Must Have |

### Investor Deck — Terminology

| ID | Requirement | Priority |
|----|-------------|----------|
| DECK-25 | No em dashes may appear anywhere in investor-deck.html | Must Have |
| DECK-26 | The Shield Four-Gate Assessment must refer to "Capital Gate" (not "Lender Gate") throughout the deck | Must Have |

## Deprecated Features

### Marketplace Model
- Removed in: v1.7.7
- Last available in: v1.7.6 — see [code-backups/v1.7.6/snapshot/](../v1.7.6/snapshot/)
- Reason for removal: PaySick adopted a vertically integrated, direct balance sheet lending model. No third-party lenders or marketplace competition. Full margin capture on every arrangement.
- Replacement: PaySick Direct Funding (balance sheet arrangement capital, Part 1 of two-part capital ask)
